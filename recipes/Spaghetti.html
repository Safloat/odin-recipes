Spaghetti Bolognese

Everyone needs a great everyday Spaghetti Bolognese recipe, and this is mine! The Bolognese Sauce is rich, thick and has beautiful depth of flavour. It’s perfect for a quick midweek meal but even better if you can simmer it for a couple of hours! Serve it over pasta, stuff into jacket potatoes, make an epic Lasagna or Baked Spaghetti Pie!

Ingredients

  1. 1 1/2 tbsp olive oil
  2. 2 garlic cloves , minced
  3. 1 onion , finely chopped (brown, yellow or white)
  4. 1 lb / 500g beef mince (ground beef)
  5. 2 beef bouillon cubes
  6. 800g / 28 oz can crushed tomato (or tomato passata)
  7. 2 tbsp tomato paste
  8. 2 tsp white sugar , if needed
  9. 2 tsp Worcestershire sauce
  10. 2 dried bay leaves
  11. 2 sprigs fresh thyme (or 1/2 tsp dried thyme or oregano)
  12. Salt and pepper
  13. 400 g / 13 oz spaghetti dried
  14. Parmesan cheese and finely chopped parsley (optional)

Method

  1. Heat oil in a large pot or deep skillet over medium high heat. Add onion and garlic, cook for 3 minutes or until light golden and softened.
  2. Turn heat up to high and add beef. Cook, breaking it up as your go, until browned.
  3. Add remaining ingredients except salt and pepper. Stir, bring to a simmer then turn down to medium so it bubbles gently. Cook for 20 – 30 minutes (no lid), adding water if the sauce gets too thick for your taste. Stir occasionally.
  4. Slow simmer option: really takes this to another level, if you have the time! Add 3/4 cup of water, cover with lid and simmer on very low for 2 – 2.5 hours, stirring every 30 minutes or so. Uncover, simmer 20 minutes to thicken sauce.
  5. Adjust salt and pepper to taste right at the end. Serve over spaghetti.
