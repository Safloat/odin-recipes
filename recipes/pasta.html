Pasta Arrabiata

Pasta Arrabiata literally means "angry pasta" in Italian. Arrabiata sauce (sugo all'arrabbiata) is a spicy (angry) tomato sauce that's made with plenty of olive oil, garlic, chopped tomatoes, and red pepper flakes to provide the heat.

Ingredients

  1. 3 tablespoons olive oil
  2. 3 large cloves garlic minced
  3. 1 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  4. 2 (13.5 fluid ounce) cans finely chopped tomatoes with juices
  5. 1 teaspoon (packed) brown sugar optional
  6. Salt & pepper to taste
  7. Freshly grated parmesan cheese
  8. â–¢8 ounces uncooked penne

Method

  1. Add the oil to a medium-sized pot over medium-high heat.
  2. Once the oil is hot, add the garlic and let it cook for a minute or so (watch it closely and don't let it brown).
  3. Stir in the red pepper flakes, tomatoes, and sugar (if using, see note). Reduce the heat and let it simmer, stirring occasionally, for about 15 minutes.
  4. While the sauce is simmering, boil a large, salted pot of water for your pasta. Cook it al dente according to package directions.
  5. Season the sauce with salt & pepper as needed. I added a fair bit of salt.
  6. Drain the pasta and toss with the sauce (you can add a little pasta water too if you wish). Top with freshly grated parmesan cheese.
